Japan The Country of Beautiful Nature

Sakura (cherry blossoms) blanket the Japanese landscape in the spring, painting the country with its gentle pink hue. These flowers are one of the country's most famous sights and have been deeply ingrained in Japanese culture: not only do Japanese people make every effort to see them in full bloom (usually between late March and April), but these flowers have made it into art and poetry, food and drink. In particular, the Japanese see their brevity - many only bloom for a week or two - as a symbol of the impermanence and transience of life. All tourists traveling through Japan in the springtime should set time aside to see Sakura.
